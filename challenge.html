THE CHALLENGE: THE KIM DYNASTY ...

Kim Il-Sung

"The Great Leader"

04/15/1912 - 07/08/1994

He started his career as as a guerilla fighter battling to free Korea from Japanese occupation in the 1930s.

With the support of the Soviet Union, he quickly amassed political power hoping to reunify Korea by force.

On June 25th, 1950 South Korea was invaded thereby igniting the Korean War.

Kim Jong-Il

"The DEAR Leader"

02/16/1941 - 12/17/2011

Kim Jong- Il began his rise through the ranks of the Korean Workers' Party.

His persona is based on a cult of personality surrounding his father.

He oversaw all aspects of the domestic film industry and made the army the central organizer of civil society to stay in power during the Arduous March.

Kim Jong-Un

"The Great Successor"

01/08/1984

He is mainly known for human rights violations and brutal suppression of opposition. After his father’s death, he removed and executed many senior officials, including his uncle.

By September 2016, the country has conducted its fifth underground nuclear test, despite a history of sanctions imposed by USA.

The Arduous March

The Soviet Union collapse during the regime of Kim Jong-Il. As a result, North Korea loses its main trading partner and with only 18% of its land suitable for farming, North Korea began experiencing a devastating famine, also known as the The Arduous March. Worried about his position in power, Kim Jong-Il instituted a “military first” policy that prioritized the military and elites over the general population. It is estimated that up to one million people died, roughly 5% of the population.

Kim Il-Sung established the Songbun System, a sociopolitical classification that determines the status of North Korean citizens based on their family’s history of perceived loyalty to the government.

This system divided the North Korean people into three groups: Core, Wavering and Hostile. Since the government controls all aspects of a person’s existence in North Korean, the songbun has an impact on access to education, housing, employment, food rationing, ability to join the ruling party and where a person is allowed to live.

The system is still in place today to punish the regime’s enemy and reward its supporters.
