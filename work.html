NKDA’S RESETTLEMENT REFUGEE PROGRAM

FLEEING THE HOMELAND

More than 25 million people live in North Korea, but their lives are very heavily controlled by the government. Every year, over 1,000 people escape from North Korea and those who get caught face harsh punishment upon repatriation, including interrogation, torture and execution.

NKDA has built 3 refugees camps along the Yalu and Tumen River to help defectors successfully apply for asylum in South Korea and Russia.

AVOIDING SEX TRAFFICKING

In 2019, 71% of defectors were women. Most of them are sold by Chinese brokers to local men to sell them into marriage or prostitution.

NDKA will rescue refugees without cost or condition while ensuring their safety on their journey to freedom. We have established relationships with individuals on the ground who can help us move refugees safely across borders.

IT COSTS $8,000/PERSON TO DEFECT FROM NORTH KOREA

Thousands of North Korean defectors have escaped their country, but are now at risk of exploitation and capture because they cannot afford the 3,000 mile journey to a safe country.

NKDA has been able to provide food, clothing, and other necessities to all defectors. However, we heavily rely on donations to cover transportation as well as accommodation.
